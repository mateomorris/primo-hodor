How much wood could a woodchuck chuck if a woodchuck could chuck wood?

This is a confusing question if you are not entirely certain of your rodent taxonomy. Yes, both woodchucks and beavers are rodents, just like rats. But beavers are semi-aquatic, whereas woodchucks are land-bound. Beavers construct their lodges out of timber, but woodchucks dig holes. This question highlights the t axiomatic confusion in suggesting woodchucks have anything to do with wood.

We could assume the question is actually about beavers, and then apply our beaver knowledge to the speculation, but not even beavers "chuck," or toss, wood. The species best known for chucking wood is the human, which is known to thrown wood significant distances in the form of javelins or arrows. But the most impressive, perhaps, is the traditional Scottish caber-tosser, which can fling a 20-foot, 150lb larch log up to sixteen times in a three-minute interval.
